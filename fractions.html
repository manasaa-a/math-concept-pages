Fractions

Three slices out of eight. What's the math way to say that?

3 / 8 That's it. Top tells you how many you have. Bottom tells you the total.

So what's a fraction, really?

A fraction is just a way of writing "part of a whole" in one neat package: a number on top, a number on bottom, a line in between.

Numerator (top) — how many parts you have. Denominator (bottom) — how many equal parts the whole is divided into.

3/8 in one picture

The whole circle is split into 8 equal slices. Three are filled. That's the entire idea — and once you see it like this, the notation becomes obvious.

The three flavours of fractions

Once you've got the basic idea, you'll see fractions show up in three forms:

3/4

Proper fraction

The numerator is smaller than the denominator — less than one whole. Like 3/4 of a pizza.

7/4

Improper fraction

Numerator equals or beats the denominator — more than one whole. 7/4 means seven quarter-slices, which is one full pizza plus 3/4 of another.

1¾

Mixed number

The same amount as 7/4, written as a whole number plus a proper fraction. Same value, two ways to write it.

Different fractions, same amount

This is the trick that makes everything else easier: 1/2 = 2/4 = 4/8. Multiply or divide top and bottom by the same number, and the fraction looks different but means exactly the same thing.
