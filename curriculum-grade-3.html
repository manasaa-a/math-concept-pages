About this curriculum

Grade 3 Math — What Students Learn

In Grade 3, students make the leap from addition and subtraction to multiplication and division — the core shift of early elementary math. They also meet fractions for the first time, learn to measure in standard units, and begin working with data.

The Cuemath Grade 3 curriculum is fully aligned to the Common Core State Standards (CCSS) and covers all seven domains: Operations & Algebraic Thinking, Number & Operations in Base Ten, Number & Operations — Fractions, Measurement & Data, and Geometry.

Frequently Asked Questions

Grade 3 covers multiplication and division facts, introduction to fractions, measurement (length, mass, volume, time), geometry (shapes, angles, lines), and data (bar graphs, line plots). The major focus is multiplication — students are expected to know all facts from 1×1 to 10×10 by the end of the year.
Yes. All lessons are mapped to the Common Core State Standards (CCSS) for Grade 3. Parents in states that use TEKS (Texas), BEST (Florida), or other state standards will find strong overlap, as these standards share most of the same content expectations.
Cuemath delivers each lesson as a live 1:1 session with a dedicated tutor, supplemented by interactive simulations and adaptive practice. The curriculum content is the same as what students cover in school — but the method is personalised, visual, and conversational rather than textbook-based.
Grade 3 has 53 lessons across 7 chapters. At 2–3 sessions per week, most students complete the grade curriculum in one academic year. Cuemath tutors adapt the pace to the individual student.
