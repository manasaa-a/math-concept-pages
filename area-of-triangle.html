Area of a Triangle

By the end of this page, you'll know how to find the area of any triangle — from base and height to all three sides.

The area of a triangle is hiding inside every rectangle.

What is the Area of a Triangle?

The area of a triangle is the 2D space enclosed by its three sides, measured in square units (cm², m², in²).

A rectangle cut diagonally from corner to corner always produces two identical triangles — each exactly half the rectangle's area. So if a rectangle has area b × h, each triangle is ½ × b × h.

What about triangles that aren't right-angled?

Cutting a rectangle diagonally always gives you two right-angled triangles — the right angle forms at the rectangle's corner. But most triangles don't have a right angle. A scalene or obtuse triangle leaning sideways doesn't fit inside a rectangle this way. For those, the connection is to a parallelogram: every triangle is exactly half a parallelogram that shares its base and height. The ½ stays — the shape you borrow from changes.

Key rule: the height must always be perpendicular to the base. It doesn't matter where the apex lands along the horizontal — only the perpendicular height counts.

How to Find the Area of a Triangle

The method depends on what information you have. Use the accordion below to find your case.

Formula
A = ½ × base × height

  • Identify the base — any side can be the base.

  • Find the perpendicular height: the distance from the opposite vertex to the base line, measured at a right angle.

  • Multiply base × height, then halve the result.

Example 1
Base = 10 cm, Height = 5 cm
A = ½ × 10 × 5 = 25 cm²
Example 2
Base = 8 cm, Height = 7 cm
A = ½ × 8 × 7 = 28 cm²
Formula
s = (a+b+c)/2  |  A = √[s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)]

  • Find the semi-perimeter: s = (a + b + c) ÷ 2.

  • Calculate each factor: (s−a), (s−b), (s−c).

  • Multiply all four: s × (s−a) × (s−b) × (s−c). Take the square root.

Example: sides 5, 6, 7
s = (5+6+7)/2 = 9
A = √[9 × 4 × 3 × 2] = √216 ≈ 14.7 cm²
Formula
A = (√3 / 4) × a²

Derived from the base–height formula: the height of an equilateral triangle with side a is (√3/2)a.

Side = 2 cm
A = (√3/4) × 4 = √3 ≈ 1.73 cm²
Side = 6 cm
A = (√3/4) × 36 = 9√3 ≈ 15.59 cm²
Formula
A = ¼ × b × √(4a² − b²)

Where a = the two equal sides, b = the base. The formula computes the height using Pythagoras first.

Equal sides a = 5, Base b = 6
Height = √(25 − 9) = 4
A = ½ × 6 × 4 = 12 cm²

The formula is (√3/4) × a². Here a = 6, so a² = 36.
Question 5 of 5

Rearrange A = ½bh: b = (2 × A) ÷ h. Substitute A = 30 and h = 6.

Key Takeaways

Area of a TriangleThe 2D space enclosed by a triangle's three sides, measured in square units.
A = ½ × b × hThe core formula. Base and perpendicular height must be at 90° to each other.
Perpendicular HeightThe vertical distance from the apex to the base line — not the slant side. This is what makes two triangles with the same base and height equal in area.
Heron's Formula√[s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)], where s = (a+b+c)/2. Used when three sides are known but no height is given.

FAQs on Area of Triangle

What is the area of a triangle?

The area of a triangle is the total 2D space enclosed within its three sides, measured in square units. It equals half the product of its base and perpendicular height: A = ½ × b × h.

Why is the area of a triangle half the area of a parallelogram?

Any triangle can be doubled by placing an identical copy alongside it — the result is always a parallelogram with the same base and height. Since the parallelogram's area is base × height, each triangle is exactly half that. This is true for every triangle, no matter its shape.

Does the position of the height change the area?

No. Only the length of the perpendicular height matters — not where it falls along the base. You can slide the apex horizontally as far as you like: as long as the height stays the same, the area stays the same. This is a key geometric fact worth remembering.

How do you find the area when you only know three sides?

Use Heron's formula. First find the semi-perimeter s = (a + b + c) ÷ 2, then calculate A = √[s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)]. This works for any triangle as long as the three side lengths form a valid triangle (the sum of any two sides must exceed the third).

What is the area of an equilateral triangle with side a?

A = (√3/4) × a². For a side of 6 cm: A = (√3/4) × 36 = 9√3 ≈ 15.59 cm². The formula is derived by finding the height using Pythagoras: h = (√3/2)a, then applying A = ½ × a × h.

How do you find the base if the area and height are known?

Rearrange A = ½bh to get b = (2 × A) ÷ h. For example, if A = 30 cm² and h = 6 cm, then b = 60 ÷ 6 = 10 cm.

Can the area of a triangle be negative?

No. Area is always a positive quantity (or zero for a degenerate triangle where all points are collinear). In coordinate geometry, the signed area formula can give a negative number to indicate vertex orientation, but we always take the absolute value for the actual area.

What units is area measured in?

Area is measured in square units. If the base and height are in cm, the area is in cm². If in metres, it's m². Always square the unit label when writing the answer: 25 cm², not 25 cm.
