Area of Parallelogram

By the end of this page, you'll know the area of parallelogram formula — and why the slant never matters.

Every parallelogram hides a rectangle — and that rectangle reveals its area.

What is the Area of a Parallelogram?

Think about it

Push a rectangle sideways — it becomes a parallelogram. The top and bottom edges stay the same length. The height stays the same. Has any area been gained or lost? Imagine cutting the triangle off one end and sliding it to the other side. This is the visual proof behind the area of parallelogram formula.

The area of a parallelogram is the space enclosed within its four sides, measured in square units. A parallelogram has two pairs of parallel sides — opposite sides are equal in length.

The key insight: slice the triangle off one slanted end and move it to the other side — you get a perfect rectangle with the same base and height. That's why A = base × height, every time.

Mini-check

A rectangle is 9 cm wide and 4 cm tall. Now push its top edge 5 cm to the right — it becomes a parallelogram. What is its area? (It's the same as the rectangle.)

Area of a Parallelogram Formula

Formula
A = base × height (h must be perpendicular to the base)
Area = b × h

  • Identify the base — any side can serve as the base.

  • Find the perpendicular height: the distance between the base and the opposite parallel side, measured at a right angle. This is never the slant side.

  • Multiply base × height.

Example 1
Base = 10 in, Height = 5 in
A = 10 × 5 = 50 in²
Example 2
Base = 20 in, Height = 8 in
A = 20 × 8 = 160 in²
Finding the other altitude
Area = 50 in², base = 6 in →
Height to 6-in side = 50 ÷ 6 ≈ 8.3 in
Formula
A = a × b × sin(θ)

Use this when you know two side lengths but not the perpendicular height. The angle θ is the one formed between the two sides.

Example: sides 4 and 6, angle 30°
A = 4 × 6 × sin(30°)
= 24 × 0.5 = 12 sq units
Special case: θ = 90° (rectangle)
A = 4 × 6 × sin(90°)
= 24 × 1 = 24 sq units
Rearranged formula
h = Area ÷ base

If you know the area and one side, you can always find the corresponding height. This is useful in reverse problems.

Example 1
Area = 2500 in², base = 250 in
h = 2500 ÷ 250 = 10 in
Example 2
Area = 420 m², height = 60 m
base = 420 ÷ 60 = 7 m
Formula
A = ½ × d₁ × d₂ × sin(x)

Where d₁ and d₂ are the two diagonal lengths, and x is the angle at which they intersect. Most useful for rhombuses, where the diagonals are perpendicular (sin 90° = 1).

Rhombus with perpendicular diagonals 8 and 6
A = ½ × 8 × 6 × sin(90°) = ½ × 48 = 24 sq units

Practice Questions on Area of Parallelogram

Question 1 of 5
What is the area of this parallelogram?
Use A = base × height. The green dashed line is the perpendicular height — not the slant side.
Question 2 of 5
A parallelogram has a base of 12 cm and a perpendicular height of 7 cm. What is its area?
A = base × height = 12 × 7.
Question 3 of 5
A parallelogram has area 96 cm² and base 12 cm. What is its perpendicular height?
Rearrange A = b × h: h = A ÷ b = 96 ÷ 12.
Question 4 of 5
A farmer's parallelogram-shaped field has a base of 400 m and a height of 250 m. What is the area in hectares? (1 hectare = 10,000 m²)
First find the area in m²: 400 × 250. Then divide by 10,000 to convert to hectares.
Question 5 of 5
A parallelogram has height twice its base. Its area is 72 cm². What is the length of the base?
Let base = b, so height = 2b. Then A = b × 2b = 2b² = 72. Solve for b.

Key Takeaways

Term / FormulaWhat it means
ParallelogramA four-sided figure with two pairs of parallel sides. Opposite sides are equal. Rectangles, rhombuses, and squares are all special parallelograms.
A = b × hArea of parallelogram = base × perpendicular height. The slant side is irrelevant — only the perpendicular distance between the two parallel sides matters.
Perpendicular HeightThe distance measured at a right angle from the base to the opposite side. Always shorter than or equal to the slant side.
A = ab sin(θ)Alternative formula using two adjacent sides (a, b) and the included angle θ. When θ = 90°, this reduces to the rectangle formula A = ab.
A = ½ d₁d₂ sin(x)Diagonal formula — used when both diagonal lengths and their intersecting angle are known. For a rhombus, x = 90° and sin(x) = 1.
