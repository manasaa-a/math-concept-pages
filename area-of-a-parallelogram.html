One more thing

What about a rhombus — or a rectangle?

Both are special cases of a parallelogram. The same formula applies.

Think about it

A rectangle has base 7 cm and height 5 cm. Now tilt it slightly. The base and height stay the same. Does the area change?

24 cm². A rhombus is a parallelogram with equal sides. Area = base × perpendicular height = 6 × 4 = 24 cm². Not 6 × 6 = 36 cm² — that would only work if the sides were perpendicular (i.e., if it were a square).

Quick reference

Key takeaways

ParallelogramA quadrilateral with two pairs of parallel sides. Opposite sides are equal.
BaseAny side chosen as the reference. Typically the bottom side.
Perpendicular heightThe shortest distance between the base and the opposite parallel side — measured at 90°. Never the slant side.
Areabase × perpendicular height. A parallelogram can always be rearranged into a rectangle with the same base and height — so they share the same formula.
Common mistakeUsing the slant side as the height. Always use the perpendicular height.
