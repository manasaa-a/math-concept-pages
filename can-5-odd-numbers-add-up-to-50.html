Can 5 odd numbers add up to 50?

Parity · Puzzle · Grades 3–5

It's impossible — and here's why.

Any odd number + any odd number = even number. Always.
Odd+Odd=Even · Even+Odd=Odd
So: (Odd + Odd) + (Odd + Odd) + Odd
= Even + Even + Odd
= Even + Odd
= Odd

Five odd numbers always sum to an odd number. 50 is even — so five odd numbers can never reach it.

The idea behind the puzzle

Odd and even — the parity rule

Odd numbers
1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11…
Can't be split into two equal whole groups. Always end in 1, 3, 5, 7, or 9.
Even numbers
2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12…
Split perfectly into two equal groups. Always end in 0, 2, 4, 6, or 8.
Think about it

What happens when you add two odd numbers? Try a few: 1 + 3, 5 + 7, 9 + 11. Is the answer always odd, always even, or does it depend?

1 + 3 = 4even
5 + 7 = 12even
9 + 11 = 20even
3 + 15 = 18even

Odd + Odd = Even. Always. No exceptions. It doesn't matter which odd numbers you pick.

Now the puzzle uses five odd numbers. Let's see what happens step by step:

Odd + Odd= Even
Even + Odd= Odd
Odd + Odd= Even
Even + Odd (5th number)= Odd ← always
Five odd numbers always add up to an odd number. 50 is even. So the answer to the puzzle is: No — it is impossible, no matter which five odd numbers you choose.

Quick reference

Key takeaways

Odd numberA whole number that cannot be split into two equal whole groups: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9… Always ends in 1, 3, 5, 7, or 9.
Even numberA whole number that can be split into two equal groups: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10… Always ends in 0, 2, 4, 6, or 8.
Odd + OddAlways even. No exceptions. Example: 7 + 9 = 16 (even).
Odd + EvenAlways odd. Example: 5 + 4 = 9 (odd).
Sum of 5 oddsAlways odd. (O+O)+(O+O)+O = E+E+O = E+O = Odd. So five odd numbers can never sum to an even number like 50.
ParityThe property of being odd or even. Used to prove things impossible — without computing every single case.
