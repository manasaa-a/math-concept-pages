Pythagorean Theorem

By the end of this page, you'll understand a² + b² = c², how to find any missing side of a right triangle, how to recognise Pythagorean triples, and why the theorem is true.

Every right triangle hides a perfect square relationship — and it works whether you're building a ramp or navigating a ship.

Think about it

A builder needs to check that a wall meets the floor at exactly 90°. She measures 3 m along the floor and 4 m up the wall. Then she checks the diagonal distance between those two points. What should it measure if the angle is truly 90°?

In any right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides.

a² + b² = c²
where c is the hypotenuse (side opposite the right angle)

The hypotenuse is always the longest side of a right triangle — the one directly opposite the 90° angle. Pythagoras of Samos (570–495 BC) formalised this theorem, though Babylonian clay tablets show the relationship was understood over 1,000 years earlier.

Mini-check

A square has side length 5 cm. What is the length of its diagonal? (Hint: the diagonal splits the square into two right triangles.)

Where this theorem appears
🏗️
Construction

A ramp rising 6 m over a horizontal run of 8 m has a slope length of exactly 10 m — a 3-4-5 triple scaled by 2.

🛳️
Navigation

A ship travels 12 km east then 5 km north. The direct distance back to port: √(144+25) = 13 km.

📡
Signal Towers

Engineers calculate a mobile tower's maximum coverage radius from its height using the theorem.

🖥️
Screen Sizes

A "55-inch TV" is 55 inches diagonally — the diagonal is the hypotenuse of the width-height right triangle.

Core Rules

The formula rearranges three ways — one for each unknown side.

Formula
c = √(a² + b²)

Square both legs, add the results, then take the square root. The hypotenuse is always the result.

Example 1 — 3-4-5
a=3, b=4 → c = √(9+16) = √25 = 5
Example 2 — 5-12-13
a=5, b=12 → c = √(25+144) = √169 = 13
Formula
a = √(c² − b²)   (rearranged from a² = c² − b²)

When the hypotenuse and one leg are known, subtract leg² from hypotenuse² and take the square root.

Example 1
c=13, b=5 → a = √(169−25) = √144 = 12
Example 2
c=17, b=8 → a = √(289−64) = √225 = 15
Converse
If a² + b² = c², the triangle is right-angled at the vertex opposite side c.

The converse works both ways: if three sides satisfy the equation, the triangle must contain a 90° angle. Builders use this to verify right angles — measure 3 m, 4 m, 5 m and if the diagonal is exactly 5 m, the corner is square.

Right triangle ✓
6, 8, 10 → 36+64=100=10² ✓ right-angled
Not right-angled ✗
5, 6, 8 → 25+36=61 ≠ 64=8² ✗

Pythagorean Triples

A Pythagorean triple is a set of three positive integers that satisfy a² + b² = c². Any whole-number multiple of a triple is also a triple.

Triple (a, b, c)CheckCommon multiples
(3, 4, 5)9 + 16 = 25 ✓(6,8,10) · (9,12,15) · (12,16,20)
(5, 12, 13)25 + 144 = 169 ✓(10,24,26)
(8, 15, 17)64 + 225 = 289 ✓(16,30,34)
(7, 24, 25)49 + 576 = 625 ✓(14,48,50)

The 3-4-5 triple is the most commonly used in construction and the simplest to remember.

Explore the Theorem

Adjust the two legs — the hypotenuse and the three squares update instantly to show why a² + b² = c².

3² + 4² = 5.00²
9 + 16 = 25

💡 Try a=3, b=4 → c=5. Then a=5, b=12 → c=13. These are Pythagorean triples — c comes out as a whole number. Notice the three squares always balance: a² + b² = c².

Practice

Question 1 of 5
A right triangle has legs 3 and 4. What is the hypotenuse?
Use a² + b² = c². What is 3² + 4²?
Question 2 of 5
A 13 m ladder leans against a wall. Its base is 5 m from the wall. How high up the wall does it reach?
The ladder is the hypotenuse (13 m). Use b = √(c² − a²) = √(169 − 25).
Question 3 of 5
Which of these is NOT a Pythagorean triple?
Check each: does a² + b² = c²? Test (4, 6, 7): 16 + 36 = 52. Is 52 = 49?
Question 4 of 5
A TV screen is 48 cm wide and 36 cm tall. What is the diagonal screen size?
The diagonal is the hypotenuse. Calculate 48² + 36².
Question 5 of 5
A right triangle has hypotenuse 17 and one leg 8. What is the other leg?
Use a = √(c² − b²). What is 17² − 8²?

Key Takeaways

TermDefinition
Pythagorean TheoremIn any right triangle: a² + b² = c², where c is the hypotenuse.
HypotenuseThe longest side of a right triangle, always opposite the 90° angle.
Pythagorean TripleThree positive integers satisfying a² + b² = c². Examples: (3,4,5), (5,12,13), (8,15,17).
ConverseIf a² + b² = c² for three sides, the triangle is right-angled at the vertex opposite c.
Finding a LegIf hypotenuse and one leg are known: a = √(c² − b²).
Applies toRight-angled triangles only. For obtuse triangles c² > a²+b²; for acute triangles c² < a²+b².
