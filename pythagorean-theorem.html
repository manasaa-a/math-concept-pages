Pythagorean Theorem

By the end of this page, you'll understand the formula a² + b² = c², how to find any missing side of a right triangle, how to recognise Pythagorean triples, and why the theorem works.

What is the Pythagoras Theorem?

The Pythagoras theorem states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides: c² = a² + b². It applies only to right-angled triangles.

Think about it

A builder checks a wall meets the floor at exactly 90°. She measures 3 m along the floor and 4 m up the wall. What should the diagonal measure if the angle is truly 90°?

Pythagoras Theorem Formula

The theorem can be visualised geometrically: the area of the square on the hypotenuse always equals the combined area of the squares on the other two sides.

Three ways to use the formula

Formula
c = √(a² + b²)

Square both legs, add the results, then take the square root.

a=3, b=4
c = √(9+16) = √25 = 5
a=5, b=12
c = √(25+144) = √169 = 13
Formula
a = √(c² − b²)

When the hypotenuse and one leg are known, subtract leg² from hypotenuse² and take the square root.

c=13, b=5
a = √(169−25) = √144 = 12
c=17, b=8
a = √(289−64) = √225 = 15
Converse
If a² + b² = c², the triangle has a right angle at the vertex opposite side c.

If the sum of the squares of any two sides equals the square of the third (largest) side, the triangle is right-angled. Builders use the 3-4-5 rule to verify right angles.

Right-angled ✓
6, 8, 10 → 36+64 = 100 = 10² ✓
Not right-angled ✗
5, 6, 8 → 25+36 = 61 ≠ 64 ✗

Pythagoras Theorem Proof

There are over 370 known proofs of the Pythagoras theorem. Here is the key insight behind the most visual one.

Arrange four congruent right triangles (each with legs a and b and hypotenuse c) inside a large square of side (a + b). They leave a tilted square of side c in the middle.

Key step
(a + b)² = 4 × (½ab) + c²
a² + 2ab + b² = 2ab + c²
∴ a² + b² = c²

Drag the points to change the dimensions of the right triangle. Notice how the total area of the two smaller squares always equals the area of the square on the hypotenuse.

💡 Watch how a² + b² = c² remains true for every right triangle.

Drop a perpendicular from the right-angle vertex to the hypotenuse. This creates two smaller triangles, each similar to the original. From the similarity ratios:

Result
AD × AC = AB²   and   CD × AC = BC²
Adding: AB² + BC² = AC(AD + DC) = AC²

Pythagoras Theorem Triangles

Right triangles that follow the rule of the Pythagoras theorem are called Pythagorean theorem triangles. When all three sides are positive integers, the set is called a Pythagorean triple.

Triple (a, b, c)VerificationCommon multiples
(3, 4, 5)9 + 16 = 25 ✓(6, 8, 10) · (9, 12, 15)
(5, 12, 13)25 + 144 = 169 ✓(10, 24, 26)
(8, 15, 17)64 + 225 = 289 ✓(16, 30, 34)
(7, 24, 25)49 + 576 = 625 ✓(14, 48, 50)

The 3-4-5 triple is the most commonly used in construction — measure 3 m and 4 m from a corner and if the diagonal is exactly 5 m, the angle is 90°.

Pythagorean Theorem Examples

Given: A right triangle with sides 8 units and 6 units. Find the hypotenuse.

Solution
c² = 8² + 6² = 64 + 36 = 100
c = √100 = 10 units

Given: A 13-foot ladder leans against a wall. The window it reaches is 12 feet high. How far is the base from the wall?

Solution
b² = c² − a² = 13² − 12² = 169 − 144 = 25
b = √25 = 5 feet

Given: Hypotenuse = 16 units, one side = 8 units. Find the third side.

Solution
16² = B² + 8²
256 = B² + 64
B² = 192
B = √192 ≈ 13.86 units

Real world examples
🏗️
Construction

A ramp rising 6 m over a horizontal run of 8 m has a slope length of exactly 10 m — a 3-4-5 triple scaled by 2.

🛳️
Navigation

A ship travels 12 km east then 5 km north. The direct distance back to port: √(144+25) = 13 km.

📡
Signal Towers

Engineers calculate a mobile tower's coverage radius from its height using the theorem.

🖥️
Screen Sizes

A "55-inch TV" is 55 inches diagonally — the diagonal is the hypotenuse of the width-height right triangle.

Key Takeaways

TermDefinition
Pythagoras TheoremIn any right triangle: a² + b² = c², where c is the hypotenuse (the longest side).
HypotenuseThe longest side of a right triangle, always opposite the 90° angle.
Pythagorean TripleThree positive integers (a, b, c) satisfying a² + b² = c². Examples: (3,4,5), (5,12,13), (8,15,17).
ConverseIf a² + b² = c² for three sides, the triangle is right-angled. Used to verify right angles in construction.
Finding a Lega = √(c² − b²) — subtract the known leg² from hypotenuse², then take the square root.
Applies toRight-angled triangles only. For obtuse triangles c² > a²+b²; for acute triangles c² < a²+b².

FAQs on Pythagoras Theorem

The Pythagoras theorem states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides: c² = a² + b².
If the sum of the squares of any two sides of a triangle is equal to the square of the third (largest) side, then it is a right-angled triangle. This is the converse of the Pythagoras theorem.
No — the Pythagorean theorem can only be applied to right-angled triangles. For other triangle types, different rules apply (cosine rule, etc.).
Hypotenuse² = Base² + Height², also written as c² = a² + b², where c is the hypotenuse and a, b are the other two legs.
The Pythagoras theorem is used in air and sea navigation to calculate the shortest distance between two points using two known distances — the perpendicular east-west and north-south distances.
It enables calculation of unknown sides in right-angled triangles and has practical applications in architecture, construction, engineering, and navigation. When any two values of a right triangle are known, the third can be found.
Three positive integers (a, b, c) satisfying a² + b² = c². The simplest and most famous is (3, 4, 5). Any whole-number multiple of a Pythagorean triple is also a triple.
Yes. For a box with dimensions l, w, h, the space diagonal is d = √(l² + w² + h²). This is a natural extension of the theorem to three dimensions.
